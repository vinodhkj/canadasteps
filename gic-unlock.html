Unlocking Your GIC Funds in Canada

Learn how GIC activation usually works after arrival, what documents to bring to the bank, and what not to assume about access to funds.

Always confirm your bank's exact appointment, document, and payout requirements directly with the bank.

Last reviewed: May 2026
Sources: IRCC
Before your bank appointment
  • A GIC is not the same as a regular chequing account — they work differently.
  • Your funds may not be available immediately after landing.
  • Most students need an in-person identity verification appointment to activate their GIC.
  • Payout timing and schedule depend on your bank's specific GIC program.
  • Do not plan rent or large payments assuming GIC funds will be accessible on your first day.

01 What a GIC is

  • →GIC stands for Guaranteed Investment Certificate.
  • →Many international students use a GIC to show proof of funds when applying for a study permit through programs such as the Student Direct Stream.
  • →The GIC is usually held by a Canadian bank before you arrive in Canada.
  • →After arrival, the GIC is activated through an identity verification process at the bank.
  • →Funds are then released according to the bank's program schedule — not all at once in most cases.

This page covers the activation and access process. It is not investment advice or financial guidance.

02 Why funds may not be available right away

Identity verification must be completed first
The bank needs to confirm your identity in person before releasing any funds. This usually requires original documents.
Your bank account may need to be opened or activated
GIC funds are typically paid into a student chequing account. If that account is not yet active, payments cannot arrive.
The GIC may need to be linked to your account
Depending on the bank, your GIC certificate may need to be formally connected to your new Canadian account during your appointment.
Internal processing may take additional time
Even after your appointment, the bank may require additional processing time before the first payment appears.
Payout schedule depends on the bank's program rules
Banks structure their GIC programs differently. The timing and frequency of payments varies — confirm this directly with your bank.

03 Before you visit the bank

  • Passport — bring your original passport for identity verification.
  • Study permit — bring the original document showing your status in Canada.
  • GIC certificate or welcome letter — bring the original bank-issued GIC document you received before arrival.
  • Bank appointment confirmation — keep the confirmation handy for branch check-in.
  • Canadian phone number if available — may be needed for account setup and online banking access.
  • Canadian address or temporary address — even a temporary address is usually acceptable for initial account setup.
  • School admission or enrolment document if requested — some banks may ask for student confirmation.
Bring original documents where possible. Banks may not accept screenshots or unclear photocopies.

04 Steps in order

  1. 01
    Read your bank's GIC welcome letter carefully
    The welcome letter usually explains what documents to bring, how to book an appointment, and what to expect at your first visit.
  2. 02
    Book a branch appointment if required
    Many banks require a scheduled appointment for GIC activation. Book as early as possible after arrival — appointments may not be available immediately.
  3. 03
    Set up a Canadian phone number if you can
    A Canadian phone number may be needed for account alerts, online banking, and security verification. Getting one before your bank visit may simplify setup.
  4. 04
    Bring original identity and immigration documents
    Bring your passport, study permit, and GIC certificate in original form. Do not rely on photos or screenshots.
  5. 05
    Open or activate your student chequing account
    If you do not already have an active chequing account at this bank, the appointment will usually include opening one. GIC payments typically deposit into this account.
  6. 06
    Ask the bank how and when the first GIC payment will arrive
    Ask specifically about the first payment — when to expect it and what account it will go into. Do not assume it will appear the same day.
  7. 07
    Confirm whether monthly payments are automatic
    Some programs release payments automatically on a set schedule. Others may require action on your part. Confirm this at your appointment.
  8. 08
    Save copies of bank agreements and account details
    Keep the account agreement, GIC release terms, and branch contact information in a safe place.

05 Questions to ask bank staff

  • Is my student account fully active now?
  • When should I expect the first GIC payment?
  • Will future payments arrive monthly or on another schedule?
  • Are there any monthly account fees I should know about?
  • Are there fees or limits for Interac e-Transfer?
  • Do I need to update my phone number or address if they change?
  • Who should I contact if the first payment does not arrive?

06 Common mistakes

  • Assuming all GIC funds are available immediately

    Funds are usually released on a schedule, not all at once. Confirm the payout terms with your bank before making plans.

  • Confusing debit card activation with GIC payout

    Your debit card may work right away, but that does not mean GIC funds have arrived. These are separate things.

  • Forgetting to bring the GIC welcome letter

    The bank may need the original GIC document to process your activation. A screenshot is often not sufficient.

  • Bringing only screenshots instead of originals

    Bring original documents for identity and immigration verification where possible.

  • Waiting too long to book a branch appointment

    Appointments may fill up, especially at the start of a semester when many students are arriving at the same time.

  • Not asking about monthly fees or transfer limits

    Student accounts often have conditions. Ask what fees apply and what transaction limits exist before you rely on the account.

  • Planning urgent payments before confirming payout timing

    Do not commit to rent or large payments on a date before you have confirmed that GIC funds will actually be available.

  • Ignoring bank emails or appointment instructions

    Banks often send preparation instructions before the appointment. Read them carefully — missing a required document can delay activation.
