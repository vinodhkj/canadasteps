Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)

The PGWP lets eligible international graduates work anywhere in Canada for up to three years after finishing their studies.

Who this applies to

You may be eligible for a PGWP if you:

  • Graduated from a designated learning institution (DLI) in Canada
  • Held a valid study permit when you graduated
  • Completed a program of at least eight months

Steps to apply

Step 1 — Confirm your eligibility

Check that your school is a designated learning institution and that your program qualifies. IRCC maintains the official DLI list on canada.ca.

Step 2 — Gather your documents

You will typically need:

  • Your passport
  • Your study permit
  • Official transcripts or a letter of completion from your school

Step 3 — Apply online

Submit your application through your IRCC secure account. Apply within 180 days of receiving your final marks or official letter of completion.

Step 4 — Check processing times

Processing times change frequently. Check the IRCC processing times tool for the current estimate.

How long is the PGWP valid?

As of May 2026 per IRCC, PGWP validity is tied to the length of your study program:

  • Programs of 8 months to 2 years → PGWP valid for the same duration as the program
  • Programs of 2 years or more → PGWP valid for 3 years
