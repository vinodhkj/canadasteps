# CanadaSteps — Advisory Agents

These are lightweight editorial and review roles, not autonomous systems. Each represents a perspective to apply when making decisions. No orchestration, no execution — just a lens.

---

## Content Reviewer

**Trigger:** Before any page is published or updated.

**Questions to ask:**
- Is every claim sourced to an official URL?
- Is the source log updated?
- Does the page follow the structure in the editorial rulebook?
- Is the reading level approximately Grade 8?
- Does the disclaimer block appear verbatim?

---

## Accuracy Critic

**Trigger:** When drafting new content or updating existing pages after an IRCC policy change.

**Questions to ask:**
- When was this page last verified against Canada.ca?
- Are any fee amounts present — and are they quoted directly from source?
- Are processing times linked to the live IRCC tool rather than stated as fixed numbers?
- Has anything on this page been superseded by a newer IRCC update?

---

## Scope Guard

**Trigger:** Whenever a new feature idea is proposed.

**Questions to ask:**
- Is this necessary for a reader to understand the current topic?
- Does this add maintenance burden without a measurable reader benefit?
- Is this solving a problem we've confirmed exists, or one we're imagining?
- Would deferring this until we have 500 monthly readers change anything?

If the answer to the last question is "no," defer it.

**Current deferred list:** calculator, chatbot, newsletter, user accounts, comments, consultant directory, CMS.

---

## UX Reviewer

**Trigger:** When a new page structure or layout change is proposed.

**Questions to ask:**
- Can a reader find the main answer within 10 seconds of landing?
- Is the page readable on a mobile phone without horizontal scrolling?
- Does every link describe where it goes?
- Are there walls of text that could be broken into a list?
- Does the page work without JavaScript?

---

## Risk Reviewer

**Trigger:** Before any new content category, feature, or operational change.

**Questions to ask:**
- Does this introduce legal or privacy exposure?
- Does this create content maintenance obligations we can sustain?
- Does this conflict with our "not legal advice" positioning?
- What breaks if we stop maintaining this in 6 months?

See `risks.md` for the live risk register.
