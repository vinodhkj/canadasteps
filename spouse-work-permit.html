Spouse Open Work Permit Guide

Understand where spouse or family work permit rules fit, what not to assume, and which official IRCC pages to verify before applying.

Rules for family open work permits change. Always verify your current situation directly with IRCC before applying.

Last verified against IRCC: May 2026
Process navigation only — not immigration advice
Read this before going further
  • A spouse or common-law partner is not automatically eligible for an open work permit — eligibility depends on the principal applicant's status and other factors.
  • Rules differ for spouses of international students, PGWP holders, and temporary foreign workers.
  • IRCC changed family open work permit rules effective January 21, 2025. Earlier information about eligibility may no longer be current.
  • Children may need separate documents depending on age, planned activities, and individual circumstances.
  • A Spouse Open Work Permit is temporary work authorization only — it does not lead to permanent residence. PR family sponsorship is a separate permanent residence pathway with its own IRCC application process and requirements.
  • Visitor visas, study permits, work permits, and PR sponsorship are different IRCC processes with different documents, timelines, and outcomes. They are not interchangeable.
  • This guide explains the process map and where to look — it does not determine or calculate eligibility for any individual situation.
Temporary status and PR sponsorship are separate Submitting a PR family sponsorship application does not automatically extend a person’s temporary status or give work rights. Visitor status, study permits, work permits, and PR sponsorship are separate IRCC processes. If you are researching permanent residence for a family member, read the Official IRCC family sponsorship page ↗.

02 Common situations people ask about

The right IRCC page to read depends on the principal applicant's current immigration status. These are brief descriptions only — verify the details with IRCC for your specific situation.

  • Spouse or common-law partner of an international student

    Some spouses of international students studying at certain institutions may be eligible to apply for an open work permit. IRCC's January 2025 rule changes narrowed which students' spouses may qualify. The current eligibility rules depend on the student's level of study and institution type. Verify current requirements directly on the IRCC page for spouses of students ↗.

  • Spouse or common-law partner of a PGWP holder

    Spouses of PGWP holders may have options depending on the PGWP holder's specific situation and the nature of their work. The January 2025 changes may affect which situations qualify. Check the IRCC page for family members of foreign workers ↗ for current rules.

  • Spouse or common-law partner of a temporary foreign worker

    Some spouses of temporary foreign workers may be eligible for an open work permit, depending on the worker's NOC level and permit type. Eligibility criteria vary. Verify the current rules on the IRCC family members page ↗ before drawing any conclusions.

  • Spouse or family member coming as a visitor

    If a spouse or partner does not qualify for a work permit, they may instead be eligible to come to Canada as a visitor. A visitor cannot work in Canada unless they hold a separate work authorization. Visitor visa requirements and eligibility are separate from work permit eligibility. Review the IRCC visitor visa guide ↗ for current process.

  • Dependent child who may need study documents

    Dependent children accompanying a parent to Canada may need their own documentation depending on their age and whether they plan to attend school. Age limits for dependent children have changed in recent years. Review the current IRCC dependent children page ↗ and the study permit guide if applicable.

03 Process map — navigating the application

  1. 01
    Identify the principal applicant's current immigration status
    Which IRCC page you need to read depends on whether the main permit holder is an international student, a PGWP holder, or a temporary foreign worker. Do not assume the same rules apply across all categories — they do not.
  2. 02
    Check the correct IRCC spouse or family page for that status
    IRCC maintains separate pages for spouses of students versus spouses of workers. Use the link that matches the principal applicant's permit type. Do not rely on third-party summaries or social media for eligibility details.
    IRCC — Spouse of a student IRCC — Family members of workers
    The January 21, 2025 rule changes affected which categories may qualify. Earlier information from before that date may no longer be accurate. See IRCC's notice on the 2025 changes ↗
  3. 03
    Confirm whether the route is an open work permit, visitor visa, or another application type
    Not every spouse situation leads to an open work permit. Some situations may lead to a closed work permit, a visitor visa, or a study permit for a child. Confirming which application type applies is a step in itself — do not assume it is an open work permit before checking.
  4. 04
    Review document requirements on the current IRCC checklist
    Document checklists differ by application type and personal situation. Use the checklist provided in the current IRCC application process — not a list from social media, a forum post, or a previous application cycle.
    IRCC checklist for spouses of students
  5. 05
    Confirm whether children need separate documents
    Children are not automatically covered by a parent's permit or visa. Depending on the child's age and plans, they may need a visitor record, study permit, or other documentation. Review this separately from the spouse's application.
    IRCC — Dependent children
  6. 06
    Apply through the official IRCC portal if eligible
    Applications are submitted through the official IRCC online portal. Applying through unofficial third-party sites may result in fraud or unnecessary fees. Use only the official IRCC account portal.
    IRCC Secure Account
    Whether someone is eligible to apply is a determination IRCC makes based on the application — this guide does not make that determination.
  7. 07
    Save submission confirmations and all IRCC correspondence
    After submitting, save the acknowledgment of receipt and any application numbers immediately. You will need these to track status or respond to IRCC requests. Do not delete IRCC emails.
  8. 08
    Track application status only through official IRCC tools
    Check application status through your IRCC online account. Processing times vary and change frequently — verify current estimates directly on the IRCC processing times page, not through third-party trackers.
    IRCC — Check application status IRCC — Processing times

04 Documents people often review

Document requirements depend on application type and individual circumstances. Use the current IRCC checklist for your specific application — not social media lists or what worked for someone else.

  • Passport — Valid passport for the spouse or partner applying. Check that it will remain valid throughout the expected permit period.
  • Marriage certificate or proof of common-law relationship — Official documentation showing the relationship between the applicant and the principal permit holder. Common-law partnerships typically require additional evidence of cohabitation.
  • Principal applicant's current study permit, work permit, or PGWP — Including all pages and any extensions. The permit type determines which IRCC page applies.
  • Proof of enrollment or employment (if requested by IRCC) — May include a letter from a Designated Learning Institution or an employer. Check the current IRCC checklist to confirm whether this applies.
  • Proof of funds (if requested) — IRCC may request evidence that the applicant can support themselves financially. Requirements vary by application type.
  • Child documents (if dependants are included) — Children may need separate documentation. Review the IRCC dependent children page for current requirements.
  • Biometrics or medical exam instructions (if requested by IRCC) — IRCC may send a Biometric Instruction Letter or request a medical exam. Follow the instructions in any IRCC correspondence.
  • Current IRCC document checklist for the specific application type — Always use the checklist from the IRCC page that matches your application. Earlier versions of checklists may be outdated.

05 Common mistakes to avoid

  • Assuming spouse eligibility is automatic

    Eligibility for a spouse open work permit is not automatic. It depends on the principal applicant's permit type, level of study or work, and other factors. Do not proceed based on an assumption — verify with IRCC.

  • Using information from before January 21, 2025

    IRCC changed family open work permit rules effective January 21, 2025. Information shared in forums, social media, or older blog posts may describe rules that no longer apply. Always check the current IRCC page.

  • Confusing visitor visa, work permit, and PR sponsorship

    These are separate application streams with different requirements, fees, and outcomes. A visitor visa does not authorize work. A work permit does not lead automatically to permanent residence. Read each IRCC page carefully for what it actually covers.

  • Assuming children are covered without checking document rules

    Children are not automatically covered by a parent's permit or visa. Check the IRCC dependent children page separately for each child, depending on their age and plans in Canada.

  • Relying only on social media advice

    Social media accounts, YouTube videos, and online forums frequently contain outdated, incorrect, or incomplete immigration information. The only reliable source for current eligibility rules is IRCC directly.

  • Submitting without reading the current IRCC checklist

    Document requirements change. Submitting an incomplete application or including wrong documents can delay processing or result in a refusal. Use the official IRCC checklist for your specific application type.

  • Ignoring biometrics or medical exam instructions

    If IRCC sends a Biometric Instruction Letter or requests a medical exam, these must be completed within the timeframe IRCC specifies. Missing these deadlines can result in an application being refused or abandoned.

  • Not saving IRCC confirmation messages

    Save all IRCC correspondence immediately after receiving it, including acknowledgment of receipt emails and application numbers. You will need these to track status and respond to any IRCC requests.
