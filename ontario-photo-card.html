Ontario Photo Card Guide for Students and Newcomers

Learn what the Ontario Photo Card is, who can apply, and what documents are commonly required.

Always verify current eligibility and document requirements directly with ServiceOntario before applying.

Last reviewed: May 2026
Source: ServiceOntario and Ontario.ca
Before you apply
  • Many newcomers prefer not to carry passports for everyday identification.
  • The Ontario Photo Card is government-issued photo identification.
  • Eligibility rules can vary depending on licence status and residency.
  • You generally cannot hold both an Ontario driver's licence and an Ontario Photo Card at the same time.
  • Original identity and immigration documents are commonly required at the time of application.

01 Why people get one

Avoid carrying a passport daily

A passport is not practical for everyday situations. The Photo Card provides local photo ID without risking your travel document.

Local photo identification for day-to-day use

Accepted for many situations where government-issued photo ID is required in Ontario.

Banking and identity verification

Banks and financial institutions often require photo ID for account setup, verification, and transactions.

Rentals and age verification

Landlords and businesses accepting age-gated services commonly ask for government-issued photo ID.

Backup identification document

Provides a secondary ID option while your passport or immigration documents are stored safely.

Easier daily carry than immigration documents

A wallet-sized card is more practical to carry than a full passport for routine daily activities.

02 Who may be eligible

Ontario residents without a valid Ontario driver's licence

The Photo Card is generally for people who do not hold an Ontario driver's licence. If you have one, you cannot also hold a Photo Card.

International students

Students living in Ontario who do not have an Ontario driver's licence may be eligible to apply.

PGWP holders

Post-Graduation Work Permit holders living in Ontario without a driver's licence may apply.

People using passports as their primary ID

Anyone currently relying on their passport for everyday identification in Ontario may benefit from applying.

Eligibility must be verified directly with ServiceOntario before applying. Rules can vary and are subject to change. This guide does not confirm eligibility for any individual situation.

03 Documents you may need

  • Passport — your current valid passport is commonly required as a primary identity document.
  • Study permit or work permit — ServiceOntario may need to see your current immigration document.
  • Proof of Ontario address — a recent utility bill, lease agreement, or other address document is commonly required.
  • Supporting immigration documents if requested — bring any additional permit or status documents in case they are needed.
  • Existing Ontario identification if applicable — bring any existing Ontario ID you hold, as it may affect what documents are required.
ServiceOntario may require original physical documents rather than copies or screenshots. Confirm document requirements directly with ServiceOntario before your visit.

04 Steps in order

  1. 01
    Confirm eligibility before visiting ServiceOntario
    Check the official Ontario.ca page and contact ServiceOntario to confirm you are eligible to apply before gathering documents or travelling to a location.
  2. 02
    Gather original identity and immigration documents
    Collect your passport, current immigration permit, and any other identity documents ServiceOntario may require. Bring originals — not copies.
  3. 03
    Confirm proof-of-address requirements
    Check what address documents ServiceOntario accepts. Requirements can vary and the official page lists acceptable formats.
  4. 04
    Visit a participating ServiceOntario location
    Not all ServiceOntario locations offer the Photo Card. Confirm your chosen location provides this service before travelling.
  5. 05
    Complete photo and identity verification
    ServiceOntario staff will take your photo and verify your documents at the location. A fee applies — confirm the current amount on the official page before your visit.
  6. 06
    Keep temporary paperwork safely until the card arrives
    You will receive a temporary document after applying. Keep it safely as your card is mailed to your Ontario address.
  7. 07
    Update records if your address changes later
    If you move, update your address with ServiceOntario. Your card is linked to your registered Ontario address.

05 Common mistakes

  • Assuming the card replaces immigration status documents

    The Ontario Photo Card is local identification only. It does not replace your study permit, work permit, passport, or any immigration document.

  • Bringing photocopies instead of originals

    ServiceOntario typically requires original physical documents. Copies, screenshots, and digital files are commonly not accepted.

  • Forgetting proof of address

    Address verification is a standard part of the application. Confirm what documents ServiceOntario accepts before your visit.

  • Confusing the Photo Card with a driver's licence

    The Photo Card is identification only. It does not permit you to drive. If you want to drive, see the Ontario Driver's License guide.

  • Assuming all ServiceOntario locations offer the same services

    Not every ServiceOntario location provides Photo Card applications. Confirm your chosen location offers this service before travelling.

  • Carrying expired immigration documents

    ServiceOntario will review your current immigration status documents. Expired documents may affect your application.

  • Applying without checking eligibility first

    If you hold an Ontario driver's licence, you are generally not eligible for a Photo Card. Check current eligibility rules before visiting ServiceOntario.
